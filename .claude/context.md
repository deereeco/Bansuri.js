### Universal Step Pattern for Indian note to western interval on the music scale
All bansuri flutes follow the same fingering pattern, regardless of key:

| Holes Closed | Indian Note | Western Interval | Semitones from Sa |
|--------------|-------------|------------------|-------------------|
| ● ● ● ○ ○ ○ | Sa          | Tonic (The Key)  | 0                 |
| ● ● ○ ○ ○ ○ | Re          | Major 2nd        | +2                |
| ● ○ ○ ○ ○ ○ | Ga          | Major 3rd        | +4                |
| ○ ○ ○ ○ ○ ○ | Ma (Tivra)  | Augmented 4th    | +6                |
| ● ● ● ● ● ● | Pa          | Perfect 5th      | +7                |
| ● ● ● ● ● ○ | Dha         | Major 6th        | +9                |
| ● ● ● ● ○ ○ | Ni          | Major 7th        | +11               |

**Key Insight**: This pattern creates a Lydian scale with all natural (non-half-hole) fingerings.
